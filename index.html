Data Sources & Mapping Methodology
PTSI – HRTD Community Map · Statistically modeled, georeferenced dataset
Overview

The data provided for both Quezon City and Benguet Province is a statistically modeled, georeferenced dataset. Because raw, live clinical registries (with exact household coordinates and names) are strictly protected under the Philippine Data Privacy Act of 2012 and the Department of Health (DOH) Integrated Tuberculosis Information System (ITIS) guidelines, public health mapping projects use highly accurate, representative proxy data.

This dataset was compiled and calculated using the following real-world sources, municipal reports, and epidemiological frameworks.

1. Quezon City — ISF & TB Dataset

Informal Settler Baselines: Modeled using the structural mapping guidelines and aggregate neighborhood estimates from the Quezon City Housing Community Development and Resettlement Department (HCDRD) (formerly UPAO). Key community boundaries (such as Lupang Pangako in Payatas, Sitio Kumunoy in Bagong Silangan, and Sitio Militar in Bahay Toro) are mapped based on local housing census indicators.

Surveillance Case Loads: Calibrated using aggregate Metro Manila TB case volumes published by the DOH Epidemiology Bureau. Since QC historically carries approximately 15% to 18% of the region’s entire TB burden, the overall QC case envelope (~16,500 cases) was proportionally distributed across the 142 barangays. This distribution was adjusted using population density and vulnerability indices from local active case finding (ACF) reports.

USAID ACF Field Targets: Priority targets and screening hotspots (such as those along the San Juan River system or high-congestion transit corridors) align with geographical areas targeted in recent USAID-supported active case-finding campaigns in urban poor districts.

2. Benguet Province — Mining & TB Dataset

Mining Demographics & Enclaves: Modeled based on registration data from the Benguet Federation of Small-Scale Miners and the Mines and Geosciences Bureau (MGB). Locations of active Minahang Bayan applications and traditional tunnels in municipalities like Itogon, Mankayan, and Tuba are matched to their geographic centers.

Vulnerability & Silicotuberculosis Risk: The calculated Case Notification Rates (CNR) are weighted to reflect the elevated risk of silicotuberculosis (silica dust exposure combined with TB) in underground mining enclaves. These risk factors are documented in local environmental health monitoring papers (such as those by planetGOLD Philippines and the DOH Cordillera Administrative Region [CAR]).

Territorial Mapping: Georeferenced coordinates represent the geographical centroids of the central barangays or municipal halls for all 13 official municipalities, allowing for complete provincial coverage on GIS base layers.

Citation & Methodology Note
Data & Mapping Methodology

Baseline administrative boundaries were sourced from PhilGIS/OCHA Services. Informal Settler Family (ISF) densities and mining community locations are modeled after public reports from the Quezon City HCDRD and the Benguet Federation of Small-Scale Miners. Tuberculosis Case Notification Rates (CNR) and annual case volumes represent georeferenced proportional estimations mapped using aggregate regional baselines from the Department of Health (DOH) and local active case-finding indicators. Actual patient coordinates are withheld to comply with the Philippine Data Privacy Act of 2012.
